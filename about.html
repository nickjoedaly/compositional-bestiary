Who I Am

About the Author

Nicholas Daly — composer, researcher, nonprofit strategist.

Nicholas Daly is a composer, researcher, and nonprofit strategist whose work moves between music, philosophy, technology, and international development. He has spent thirty-five years as a working musician across oral traditions on four continents, and is now crossing into the written tradition through private composition study — a journey that is the biographical origin of the Bestiary itself.

His master's research, conducted across three European universities through the Erasmus Mundus program, examined technology as pharmakon (through Bernard Stiegler) and the phenomenology of perception (through David Abram), producing a thesis on ecophenomenology and the ambivalence of technical mediation. A parallel study on Harry Partch's tuning systems, written for Paulo Ferreira de Castro at NOVA Lisboa, applied Bakhtin's chronotope to twentieth-century American musical avant-garde.

As a Fulbright scholar in Spain, he worked within the Spanish education system while deepening the linguistic and cultural fluencies that later carried through positions at global institutions including the Institute for New Economic Thinking (Soros-founded), MIMA Music (UNESCO-affiliated), and the Montana Conservation Corps. His nonprofit consulting practice has secured over $21 million in capital grants.

He is currently studying composition with Bohdan Syroyid Syroyid and developing the Compositional Bestiary as a doctoral research project at the Universidad de Salamanca — building, at last, the tools he wished he'd had for the first thirty-five years.
