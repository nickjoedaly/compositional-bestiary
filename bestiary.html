A Catalog of Creatures

The Bestiary

A catalog of compositional creatures, each encoding a philosophical position about music.

01What is a Creature?

A creature is a rule system. A creature is a constraint. A creature is a philosophy made interactive—a way of thinking about music that can be instantiated as a MuseScore plugin, a game, a listening exercise, or a composition prompt.

Each creature descends from a lineage of thinkers, artists, and inventors who understood that constraint is not a limitation but a gateway. Composers like Brian Eno and John Zorn found freedom in systems. Philosophers like Pauline Oliveros transformed attention into practice. Mystics like Athanasius Kircher believed that logic itself was sacred.

The creatures in this bestiary carry forward these traditions, reimagined for collaborative composition with humans and algorithms. A creature does not compose for you. It composes with you.

03Creature Prototypes

Three creatures currently in development, each representing a different philosophical stance and technical approach.

The Muwashshaḥ Engine

Philosophy: Andalusian strophic forms as compositional constraint.

Constraints: Fixed rhyme schemes mapped to pitch relationships, creating harmonic patterns that echo poetic structure.

Notation: Standard Western notation with Arabic maqam indicators.

In Progress

The Dice Garden

Philosophy: Chance as teacher, descendent of Mozart dice games.

Constraints: Aleatory selection from pre-composed fragments with student-authored elements.

Notation: Standard Western notation with selection matrices.

Planned

The Listening Mirror

Philosophy: Oliveros-inspired deep listening and environmental phenomenology.

Constraints: Environmental sound transcription transformed into compositional material through systematic procedures.

Notation: Graphic notation and gestural score hybrid.

Planned
