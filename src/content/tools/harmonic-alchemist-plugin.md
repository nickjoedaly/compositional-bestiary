## Interval Transformation Through Philosophical Reasoning

The Harmonic Alchemist plugin brings the Harmonic Alchemist creature into MuseScore as a tool for exploring harmonic relationships through systematic transformation. Rather than applying mechanical operations like transposition or inversion, the Alchemist engages in what might be called *philosophical reasoning about consonance and dissonance*. It examines the intervals present in a selected passage and suggests transformations based on a set of harmonic principles: proximity and voice leading efficiency, resolution of tensions toward consonance, exploration of boundary cases between consonant and dissonant regions.

The plugin operates on selected notes or chords and presents the user with a set of transformation options, each explained with reasoning. For example, faced with a tritone, the Alchemist might suggest: "This interval can move toward stability through gentle stepwise motion in both voices" or "This interval might be enriched by adding a third voice that recontextualizes it within a larger harmonic field." The user retains final decision-making power but is guided by the creature's harmonic judgment.

## Usage and Sonic Imagination

The Harmonic Alchemist is designed for several compositional scenarios: exploring alternative harmonizations of a melody, generating harmonic voicings from a given set of intervals, analyzing the harmonic content of an existing passage and suggesting evolution or development. The plugin includes configurable parameters: harmonic palette (which intervals are considered consonant or dissonant in the current context), voice leading strictness (how smoothly voices should move), and exploration depth (how far afield from the original the Alchemist is willing to venture).

Beyond its practical utility, the Harmonic Alchemist embodies a particular philosophy of harmony —one that understands harmonic relationships not as timeless universal truths but as culturally and historically situated, subject to transformation through understanding and imagination. By making its reasoning transparent, the plugin invites users to engage not just with the results of harmonic transformation but with the *criteria* by which harmony is judged, potentially leading to deeper understanding of both conventional harmony and alternative harmonic approaches from other traditions.
