Doctoral Thesis

The Compositional Bestiary

AI-Enhanced Composition Pedagogy as a Technics of Inner Revelation

ABSTRACT

Thesis Summary

This practice-based doctoral thesis argues that AI-enhanced composition tools, when designed as structured encounters rather than automated generators, can function as instruments of autodidactic formation—what this thesis terms a technics of inner revelation. Drawing on a six-hundred-year lineage of enchanted composition devices (from Guido d'Arezzo's mnemonic Hand through Kircher's Arca Musarithmica to Mozart-era dice games), the thesis demonstrates that algorithmic generation has always been compatible with—even essential to—human musical self-discovery.

The central creative artifact is the Compositional Bestiary: a collection of AI-augmented compositional “creatures,” each encoding a distinct philosophical position about music, each teaching through constraint, play, and structured encounter. Where Kircher's Arca aimed at universal interchangeability, the Bestiary inverts this: each creature aims at unrepeatable singularity—the emergence of the student's own compositional voice.

The thesis is grounded in three commitments: learning as adventure, personhood as sacred, and technology as companion. Its theoretical framework draws on Corbin's mundus imaginalis, Stiegler's pharmakon, Illich's convivial tools, Biesta's subjectification, Ong's orality/literacy dialectic, and Abram's ecophenomenology.

STRUCTURE

Five Movements

The thesis follows a five-movement architecture, each chapter building on the last, from the familiar toward the visionary:

Ch. I

Conventional Didactics

Planned

A critical survey of existing composition pedagogy—its methods, assumptions, institutional contexts, and blind spots. What does the field currently offer, and where does it fail the autodidact, the cross-tradition musician, the late entrant to composed music? This chapter establishes the problem space: notation as gatekeeping, pedagogy as under-theorized, technology as directionless.

Ch. II

Historical & Cosmological Didactics

In Progress

The six-hundred-year lineage of enchanted composition tools: Guidonian Hand, Ars Magna, Arca Musarithmica, Musical Dice Games, Componium. What did these devices teach, and what cosmological assumptions did they encode? The Arca as devotional act; the dice game as democratic promise; the Componium as prophecy. This chapter argues that the history of algorithmic composition is the history of music education, not its antithesis.

Ch. III

Corbinian Autodidactics

Planned

A new epistemological framework. Drawing on Corbin's mundus imaginalis (the real-but-not-physical realm accessed through active imagination), Illich's convivial tools, Ong's orality/literacy, Biesta's subjectification, and Gordon's audiation, this chapter argues that composition pedagogy, at its deepest, is a technics of inner revelation—a practice of training the student to hear a voice that was always already present.

Ch. IV

Comparative World Music Didactics

Planned

How composition is taught across traditions: West African master-apprentice drumming, Indian guru-shishya parampara, Javanese gamelan, Irish session culture, flamenco duende. What can the Western conservatory learn from pedagogies rooted in oral transmission, embodied knowledge, and spiritual formation? This chapter provides the cross-cultural evidence for the Corbinian framework.

Ch. V

The Bestiary

In Progress

The practical instantiation: the creatures, the MuseScore plugins, the compositions, the encounters. The Bestiary as proof-of-concept, as creative portfolio, and as the convergence point where every preceding chapter finds its embodiment. The book as atlas (cf. Solnit's Unfathomable City); the atlas as world.

CORE PRINCIPLES

Three Commitments

Learning as

Adventure

Each creature is an encounter, not a lesson. The student enters the Bestiary as a traveler enters an unfamiliar landscape—alert, curious, discovering through exploration what no curriculum could prescribe.

Personhood as

Sacred

The Bestiary exists to serve the emergence of the student's own musical self—what Gert Biesta calls subjectification. The tool never overwrites. It reveals. The person is always more than the system can model.

Technology as

Companion

Neither master nor servant. The AI is a responsive, structured companion in the compositional process—a pharmakon designed for the curative orientation, asking better questions than it gives answers.

METHODOLOGY

Practice-Based Artistic Research

This is a practice-based PhD following the artistic research tradition (Frayling 1993, Borgdorff 2012). The creative work is the research. The Bestiary's compositions, plugins, and pedagogical encounters are not illustrations of a theory—they are the theory's primary evidence.

The thesis will be assessed as both a scholarly contribution and a creative portfolio, following the conventions of artistic research doctorates at European institutions and the Society for Artistic Research (SAR).
